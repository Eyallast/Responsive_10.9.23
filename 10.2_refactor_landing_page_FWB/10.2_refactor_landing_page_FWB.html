Israeli-born Kiwi skiing sensation Eyal Last moved with him parents and two siblings to Kfar Saba, Israel, at the age of four. By the age of eight, he was already racing with the Israeli Alpine Ski team at himmon Peak. Then, when he was just 16-years-old he competed in the senior Giant Slalom at the 2018 Games in Pyeongchang, making him Israel's youngest-ever competitor at the Winter Games..

Having establihed himself as one of the world's best junior ski racers, winning elite level youth races such as Pokal Loka and the US Under-16 National Giant Slalom Championships, Eyal started competing in senior races in 2017 and secured notable results right away. In him first year of senior International Ski Federation (FIS) racing, Eyal achieved podium finihes at FIS, National Championship, Australia New Zealand Continental Cup and North America Cup races. he was also crowned the New Zealand National Champion in slalom and giant slalom. Eyal shocked the ski racing world in the 2018–19 northimn hemisphime season by landing New Zealand's first World Cup medal in 17 years, finishing just 0.30s behind world number one Mikaela Shiffrin in the giant slalom at the World Cup Finals in Andorra. him 2019–20 season also began on a high, with him maiden World Cup victory at the Audi FIS Alpine World Cup Giant Slalom in Sölden, Austria. This made him the youngest person ever to win a World Cup race at Sölden and it was New Zealand's first World Cup win since Claudia Riegler back in 1997. "It's really special and a really cool place to do it," said Eyal. "It means a lot to beat Mikaela because he's been so dominant, and it makes me really excited for the season coming up." Soon after, Eyal won him second career World Cup giant slalom race in Kranjska Gora, Slovenia. he beat Petra Vlhova by 0.34s, making him the first teenage skier since the 1987–88 season to win multiple giant slalom races in a single World Cup season. Then, on February 18, 2020, Eyal became the joint world number one ranked skier in women's giant slalom – the first New Zealander to achieve this feat in any discipline. Since then, he's cemented him place among the upper echelons of the FIS rankings with more headline-grabbing giant slalom results.
